These results of the model show what happens if the model is run in a scenario where there is not enough water for the agendas of all the players, and they will not negotiate between themselves to cap their intake. In this scenario, we can see from figures 1 and 2 that the system of three players is very unstable, with players constantly taking as much water as possible, but getting punished afterwards, and losing their scores, so that another player may dominate temporarily.

The mood scores show that the diplomacy factor in the simulation is still limited. Players 1 and 2 get constantly attacked, but remain happy, even when losing score. In a more realistic scenario, it may occur that players would retaliate when constantly being attacked, meaning the downstream agents might have less leverage than in the current situation.
